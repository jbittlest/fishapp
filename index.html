📡 No signal? Satellite SOS

iPhone 14 or newer can call for help with no cell service:
  1. Try to call 911 — if there’s no signal it offers Emergency Text via Satellite.
  2. Or hold the side button + a volume button, then drag Emergency SOS.
  3. Point the phone at open sky and follow the on-screen arrow to connect.
  4. Answer the questions — it relays your location to responders.
Practice free anytime: Settings → Emergency SOS → Try Demo. Some new Android phones (Pixel 9+, Galaxy S24+) have satellite SOS too — check your Safety settings.

MAYDAY script (life-threatening)

“MAYDAY, MAYDAY, MAYDAY.
This is [boat name], [boat name], [boat name].
MAYDAY, [boat name].
My position is [coordinates above].
Nature of emergency: [sinking / fire / medical / man overboard].
Number of people on board: [#].
We require immediate assistance. Over.”

For urgent-but-not-life-threatening issues use PAN-PAN (say it 3×) instead of MAYDAY.
